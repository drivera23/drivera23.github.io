Which Graduate School Should I Attend?

Just throwing some stuff at the wall.

The breakdown is the following: Non-academic and Academic factors.

Non-academic factors: Remember, a PhD is a commitment, meaning that you will spend somewhere around 4-6 years in a high-stress environment. This means that the environment you put yourself in will dictate many aspects of your life, including your physical and mental well-being. So take a step back, and ask yourself, what does my ideal environment look like? I suggest trying to answer this list of preliminary questions:

  • Location: Is weather a big factor to you? Do you want to live somewhere that snows a lot? Do you prefer a more tropical setting?

  • Logistics: Is the place walkable? Do you need a car?

  • Personal: Do you want to be close to your family? Say, a flight away? Or maybe not, you may prefer being on the other side of the world XD.

  • Lifestyle: Do you want to live in a big city? Or would you rather live in a smaller college town?

Academic factors: I suggest trying to answer this list of preliminary questions:

  • Ranking: Is this school known at the national/international level?

  • Advisor: This one can be tricky.

  • Lab: Ask many questions

  • Resources: Do you want to live in a big city? Or would you rather live in a smaller college town?
